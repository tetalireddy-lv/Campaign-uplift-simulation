# Campaign Brief Schema

## Required Fields

Every campaign brief must contain the following structured fields:

### 1. Campaign Name
- A short, descriptive identifier for the campaign
- Should indicate industry, objective, or audience

### 2. Business Objective
- What business outcome does the campaign drive?
- Must be specific and measurable
- Examples: "Convert 200 enterprise trial accounts to paid", "Generate 1,000 MQLs"

### 3. Target Audience
- Who is the campaign aimed at?
- Should include: segment name, demographics, psychographics, behavior signals
- Must be specific enough to inform channel selection

### 4. Key Message
- The primary message or value proposition
- Should be a single clear statement
- Supporting messages are optional but helpful

### 5. Channels
- Which channels will be used?
- Each channel should have a rationale tied to audience behavior
- Common channels: Email, Paid Search, Paid Social, Display, Content/SEO, Events, Direct Mail, In-App, SMS, Webinar

### 6. Budget
- Total campaign budget
- Currency specified
- Channel-level breakdown preferred
- Must be realistic for the objective and timeline

### 7. Timeline
- Campaign start and end dates
- Key milestones (asset deadlines, launch dates, review dates)
- Measurement window

### 8. Success Metrics (KPIs)
- Primary KPI with baseline and target
- Secondary KPIs
- Measurement method and reporting cadence
- Attribution model if applicable

### 9. Constraints / Brand Notes
- Brand guidelines to follow
- Legal/compliance requirements
- Audience exclusions
- Channel restrictions
- Competitor considerations

## Quality Scoring

| Quality Level | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| High | All 9 fields present, specific, measurable, internally consistent |
| Medium | Most fields present but some vague or missing detail |
| Low | Multiple missing fields, vague objectives, no measurable targets |
| Incomplete | Fewer than 5 fields populated |
