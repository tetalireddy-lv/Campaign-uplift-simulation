# Campaign Planning Examples

## Example 1: High-Quality Brief

### Input Brief
```
Campaign Name: Q3 Enterprise Trial Conversion
Business Objective: Convert 200 enterprise trial accounts to paid subscriptions by end of Q3 2026
Target Audience: Enterprise IT decision-makers currently in 14-day trial, company size 500+, using at least 3 product features
Key Message: "Your team is already seeing results — lock in your progress before trial ends"
Channels: Email (primary), In-App (supporting), LinkedIn Ads (retargeting)
Budget: $85,000 USD — Email: $15K, In-App: $10K, LinkedIn: $45K, Creative: $15K
Timeline: June 1 - August 31, 2026. Email sequence starts Day 7 of trial.
Success Metrics: Primary: 40% trial-to-paid conversion rate (baseline: 22%). Secondary: Pipeline value $2M.
Constraints: No discount offers without VP approval. Must comply with enterprise data handling policy. No cold outreach to accounts in active sales cycle.
```

### Expected Readiness Score: High (85-95%)
### Expected Gaps: Minimal — possibly timeline milestones and asset specifics

## Example 2: Medium-Quality Brief

### Input Brief
```
Campaign Name: Summer Sale Push
Business Objective: Drive summer revenue
Target Audience: Existing customers and prospects
Key Message: Summer savings
Channels: Email, social media, website
Budget: $50,000
Timeline: June-August
Success Metrics: Increase revenue
Constraints: Follow brand guidelines
```

### Expected Readiness Score: Low-Medium (35-50%)
### Expected Gaps:
- Objective not measurable (no target number)
- Audience too broad
- Channels not specific (which social platforms?)
- No budget allocation
- Timeline has no milestones
- KPI has no baseline or target
- Constraints are generic

---

## Example 3: Incomplete Brief

### Input Brief
```
Campaign Name: New Product Launch
Business Objective: Launch the new product
Target Audience: TBD
Channels: All channels
Budget: TBD
Timeline: ASAP
Success Metrics: Awareness
```

### Expected Readiness Score: Very Low (10-20%)
### Expected Gaps: Nearly every field is missing or vague

---

## Example Clarification Questions

For the Medium-Quality Brief above:
1. "What specific revenue target are you aiming for? (e.g., $500K incremental revenue)"
2. "Can you define the customer segments? (e.g., lapsed customers vs. active vs. prospects)"
3. "Which social media platforms does your audience use most?"
4. "What is the current baseline revenue for a comparable period?"
5. "Are there specific products or categories to feature?"

## Example Assumptions

For the Medium-Quality Brief above:
1. "Assuming 'summer revenue' means incremental revenue above same-period last year"
2. "Assuming 'social media' means Instagram and Facebook based on audience demographics"
3. "Assuming $50K budget is all-in including creative production"
4. "Assuming 'existing customers' are the primary audience with prospects as secondary"
