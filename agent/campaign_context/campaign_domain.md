# Campaign Management Domain Overview

## What is a Campaign?

A campaign is a coordinated set of marketing activities designed to achieve a specific business objective within a defined timeframe, budget, and audience segment.

## Campaign Lifecycle

1. **Briefing** — Stakeholder provides objectives, audience, budget, timeline
2. **Planning** — Channel selection, asset creation, timeline workback
3. **Approval** — Manager and legal review
4. **Execution** — Assets deployed across channels
5. **Measurement** — KPIs tracked against targets
6. **Optimization** — Adjustments based on performance data

## Key Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Campaign Manager | Owns the end-to-end campaign lifecycle |
| Stakeholder | Provides business objective and budget authority |
| Creative Team | Produces campaign assets |
| Channel Specialist | Executes channel-specific tactics |
| Legal/Compliance | Reviews claims, disclosures, data usage |
| Analytics | Defines measurement plan and reports results |

## Campaign Quality Dimensions

A well-formed campaign brief should be:

- **Specific** — Clear objective with measurable target
- **Audience-defined** — Named segments with known characteristics
- **Channel-appropriate** — Channels match audience behavior
- **Time-bounded** — Start date, end date, key milestones
- **Budget-allocated** — Total budget with channel breakdown
- **Measurable** — KPIs with baselines and targets
- **Compliant** — No regulatory or brand guideline violations

## Common Campaign Failures

- Vague objectives ("increase awareness")
- No baseline for KPIs
- Budget without channel allocation
- Timeline without milestones
- Audience too broad or undefined
- Channel selection without audience fit rationale
- Missing legal/compliance review
- No measurement window defined
